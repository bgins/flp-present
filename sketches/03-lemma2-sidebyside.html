§ 3::lemma 2 · bivalent initial

P has a bivalent initial configuration.

[ paper §3 ]
▸ proof by contradiction. Suppose every initial were univalent. Then two adjacent initials (differing only in p₃'s x) would be 0-valent and 1-valent. But the same schedule σ with p₃ crashed runs identically on both — and reaches the same decision. The supposed valency assignment can't hold.
